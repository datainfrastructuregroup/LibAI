# Critical Media Literacy

In an age of AI-generated content, algorithmic curation, and information overload, critical media literacy becomes essential for democratic participation and individual autonomy.

## Understanding Media Literacy

### Traditional Media Literacy
- **Source Evaluation**: Assessing credibility and reliability
- **Bias Recognition**: Identifying perspectives and agendas
- **Fact-Checking**: Verifying claims and evidence
- **Context Understanding**: Placing information in broader context

### Digital Challenges
- **AI-Generated Content**: Deepfakes, synthetic text, and images
- **Algorithmic Curation**: Personalized content feeds
- **Information Overload**: Volume and velocity of information
- **Platform Dynamics**: How social media shapes content

## Critical Frameworks

### Media Analysis Approaches
- **Political Economy**: Understanding ownership and economic incentives
- **Cultural Studies**: Examining representation and meaning
- **Critical Theory**: Analyzing power and ideology
- **Postcolonial Analysis**: Understanding global information flows

### AI-Specific Literacy
- **Understanding AI Limitations**: Knowing what systems can and cannot do
- **Recognizing AI Content**: Identifying synthetic media
- **Algorithmic Awareness**: Understanding how content is selected
- **Data Literacy**: Understanding data collection and use

## Skills and Competencies

### Analytical Skills
- **Source Evaluation**: Assessing information credibility
- **Context Analysis**: Understanding broader social and political context
- **Bias Detection**: Identifying perspectives and assumptions
- **Evidence Assessment**: Evaluating claims and support

### Technical Skills
- **Tool Usage**: Using verification and analysis tools
- **Platform Understanding**: Knowing how media systems work
- **Privacy Protection**: Managing personal data and security
- **Digital Security**: Protecting against manipulation

## Pedagogical Approaches

### Learning Strategies
- **Inquiry-Based Learning**: Questioning and investigation
- **Critical Dialogue**: Discussion and debate
- **Media Production**: Creating media to understand systems
- **Reflective Practice**: Metacognition about media consumption

### Educational Contexts
- **Formal Education**: School and university curricula
- **Community Education**: Public workshops and programs
- **Online Learning**: Digital literacy platforms
- **Workplace Training**: Professional development

## Resistance and Alternatives

### Alternative Media
- **Independent Journalism**: Non-corporate news sources
- **Community Media**: Local and participatory media
- **Public Broadcasting**: Non-commercial media systems
- **Citizen Journalism**: User-generated news and analysis

### Digital Practices
- **Media Diversification**: Consuming diverse perspectives
- **Platform Alternatives**: Using non-mainstream platforms
- **Community Building**: Creating information communities
- **Critical Sharing**: Responsible content distribution
